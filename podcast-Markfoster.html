Mark Foster — SVP Global Business Services at IBM

During THINK 2019 I had good success in reaching out to people. Perhaps I might have got overzealous as I decided to try my luck at asking IBM CEO Ginni Rometty for an interview, because you know, you never know unless you try right? She replied (which was big enough news already) stating that she admired my tenacity but unfortunately she was booked end to end, to which I thought - fair enough. She then however introduced me over email to Mark Foster, Senior Vice President of Global Business Services at IBM, suggesting that he might be interested in accepting such a request. Awesome. I arranged a time to meet with Mark and then quickly set out to learn as much as I could before the interview. One of the first things I realised when I found Mark's blog is that he has led an extremely full and balanced life. His blogs had strong notes of family, friends, hobbies and helping others. Meeting Mark was an absolute pleasure. As someone that is personally driven to both have a successful rewarding career as well as a balance lifestyle, I found Mark particularly inspiring. From an ancient history graduate to global leader in one of the world's greatest technology companies, Mark's story is a great example that your university degree doesn't have to determine the career path you take. Mark is a super nice guy and I believe that IBM GBS is in great hands. Hope you guys enjoy the interview!

💡 Key Messages

Here are my key takeaways from the podcast.

  • Your degree doesn't have define your career - As a graduate in ancient Greek and Latin language and history, Mark is now a global leader at IBM, one of the largest and greatest technology companies in the world
  • Live a balanced life - Mark stresses that we need to have a wide range of things in our life that interest us and that when we become narrow, it is not good for us or those arounds us
  • Be planful - Mark uses his monthly blog as a way of thinking about what he has planned for next month and if it will be interesting enough to write about. This helps him make sure he plans for a variety of activities in his life
  • Inspire others - When Mark looks back on his career, he wishes he will be able to say that he has inspired those people around him, much like others had inspired him during his life journey
  • Keep on trying - If Mark could talk to a younger version of himself he would say just keep on trying. The only thing you can't be planful of is where your career will take you, so just focus on enjoying what you are doing at the time, do a good job and always look for adjacent areas to explore and maintain that curiosity
  • Read A Short History of Nearly Everything by Bill Bryson - It's a great book that makes us all realise how we sit in the in the universe. At one level how insignificant we are but also at one level how amazing we are

About Mark Foster

Mark Foster is Senior Vice President, IBM Global Business Services, responsible for the strategy, client value and business performance of IBM’s consulting, systems integration, digital agency, business process outsourcing and application management services businesses across all industries. GBS's 125,000 consultants partner with leading clients around the world to set digital strategy, reinvent business processes with AI and modernize core applications and systems on the cloud. Retired in 2011 as Group Chief Executive Global Markets and Management Consulting after 27 years at Accenture. In this role led the global management consulting practice of 15,000 and oversaw industry programs and major investments across consulting, technology and outsourcing. Previously Group Chief Executive Products Industries leading Accenture’s management consulting practices in the Retail, Consumer, Health, Transport and Industrial sectors. Member of the Executive Leadership Team; Chair of Global Markets Council; Leader of Diamond Client Council, Accenture engagement with the World Economic Forum and Accenture Development Partnerships. Chaired the WEF Global Health Initiative Against AIDS, TB and Malaria and Global Health Advisory Board, Vice Chaired the Mayor of Seoul's International Business Advisory Council. In 2011-2015 served the UK Government as founding Commissioner for the Independent Commission for Aid Impact (ICAI), overseeing the UK’s £11n aid spend globally. Served as Independent Non Executive Director on the business boards of Heidrick & Struggles, Fidessa, Computer Sciences Corporation, Alexander Mann Solutions and Atento. In the non-profit sector Chaired the International Business Leaders Forum and Peaslake Schools Trust, and served as Board Member of the Royal Shakespeare Company and Cranleigh School. Lives in Old Greenwich, CT with wife Sandy and sons Alex and Matt. Hobbies include travel, photography, history, 7-a-side football, jogging, theatre and blogging.
